## Lab 3

### Target respondents
- University students (local and international students)
- Administrative staff responsible for room booking management

### Pain Points
- Students must physically visit the office to make a reservation.
- The booking process relies on paper forms, making it time-consuming and inconvenient.
- Booking forms are available only in Thai, creating language barriers for international students.
- Students need to communicate with respective staff to reserve study spaces, causing delays and dependency on staff availability.
- There is no easy way to check room availability beforehand.
- Manual record keeping increases the risk of double bookings and human errors.

### Current alternatives
- Visit the office and fill out a paper-based room reservation form.
- Contact the responsible staff directly to request a study room.
- Ask staff for information regarding room availability.
- Use manual records maintained by the department or office.

### Consequences
- Students spend unnecessary time traveling to the office.
- International students may face difficulties understanding the booking procedure and forms.
- Room reservation becomes inefficient and dependent on office hours and staff availability.
- Delays in communication can result in missed opportunities to reserve rooms.
- Manual processes increase the likelihood of booking conflicts and administrative errors.
- Users experience lower satisfaction with the room booking process.
