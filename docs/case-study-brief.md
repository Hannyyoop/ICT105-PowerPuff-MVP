# Case Study Brief: Room Booking System

## Context
Students and student organizations often waste time trying to find available rooms on campus for meetings, study sessions, or club activities. They frequently have to physically walk to various administrative offices to check room availability and make bookings manually, which can lead to scheduling conflicts, double-bookings, and major delays.

## Target User Groups
- University students (for study groups or project work) 
- Student clubs, committees, and organizations (for events or meetings)
- Administrative staff or faculty members who manage and approve room allocations
  
## Initial Problem Assumption
Students struggle to secure spaces effectively because room availability is not visible in real time, and the booking process relies entirely on scattered, manual paperwork.

## Current Alternatives
- Walking to administrative or faculty offices manually
- Filling out physical paper request forms
- Speaking directly with staff or building security to ask if a room is empty
- Contacting staff via official email or messaging lines and waiting for a reply
  
## Possible MVP Direction
A web-based and mobile-friendly room booking platform where users can view available rooms and time slots in real time, submit online booking requests, receive instant confirmations or updates, and easily modify or cancel their reservations.

## Feasible Tools
Google Forms, Google Sheets, Figma for prototyping, HTML/CSS/JS for the website, Node.js and MySQL for the booking backend, and Firebase for login and hosting.
