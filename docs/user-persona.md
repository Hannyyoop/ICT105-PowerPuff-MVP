# Lab 04 - User Persona


## 1. Persona Name
**Amina, Third-Year Computer Science Student**

## 2. User Type
Student who frequently books study rooms for individual study and group assignments.

## 3. Background and Context
- Amina is a third-year Computer Science student.
- She regularly studies in the university library and meets her project group.
- She usually experiences the problem during exam weeks or before assignment deadlines.
- She often spends time walking around the library because she does not know which study rooms are available.
- She currently uses:
  - University LMS
  - Microsoft Teams
  - Google Calendar
  - LINE
  - University Email
## 4. Goals
- Find an available study room quickly.
- Book a study room online before arriving.
- Receive instant booking confirmation.
- Avoid booking conflicts.
- Save time searching for study spaces.


## 5. Pain Points from Lab 03 Evidence
| Pain Point | Evidence ID / Respondent | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| No real-time room availability | E01 | Students waste time searching for empty study rooms. |
| Double booking | E02 | Students arrive and find another group already using the room. |
| Manual booking process | E04 | Students must ask library staff instead of booking online. |
| Booking status is unclear | E06 | Students do not know whether their reservation is approved. |
| Difficult to find study rooms | E08 | Students walk around multiple floors looking for available rooms. |
## 6. Current Alternatives / Workarounds
| Current Alternative | Weakness / Limitation | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Walk around the library | Takes time and rooms may already be occupied | E01, E08 |
| Ask library staff | Depends on staff availability | E04 |
| Study in cafeteria | Too noisy for studying | E03 |
| Ask friends | Information may not be accurate | E07 |

## 7. Design Implications
### The system should support:
- Real-time room availability
- Online study room booking
- Automatic conflict prevention
- Booking confirmation
- Booking status tracking
- Search by date, time, capacity, and location
  
### The team should avoid:
- Manual booking procedures
- Complicated booking forms
- Delayed confirmation
- Outdated room availability

### Most important MVP requirement:
Allow students to check room availability in real time and book a study room quickly without booking conflicts.
