# Assumption-Evidence Table

| Assumptions | Evidence |
|-------------|----------|
| Students find the current room booking process inconvenient. | Students are required to physically visit the office and complete a paper-based form. |
| International students encounter language barriers during room reservations. | The booking form is available only in Thai, making it difficult for non-Thai speakers to understand the instructions. |
| The current booking process is inefficient and time-consuming. | Users must travel to the office and are limited by office hours when making reservations. |
| Dependence on staff delays study room reservations. | Students must contact the respective staff members to book study spaces, and the process depends on staff availability. |
| Users cannot easily determine room availability. | There is no centralized system that provides real-time information on room schedules and availability. |
| Manual booking procedures are susceptible to errors. | Paper-based records increase the possibility of misplaced forms, inconsistent records, and double bookings. |
| International students would benefit from a multilingual booking system. | English support would improve accessibility and reduce misunderstandings during the booking process. |
| A web-based room booking system would improve efficiency and accessibility. | Online reservations and real-time room availability would eliminate unnecessary visits to the office and reduce administrative workload. |

## Sources of Evidence

- Observation of the current room reservation procedure.
- Interviews with students and administrative staff.
- User surveys regarding satisfaction with the existing process.
- Existing paper-based booking forms and reservation procedures.
